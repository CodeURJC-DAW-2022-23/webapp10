KPI Definitions

KPI - Key Performance Indicators

Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) are metrics used to measure an organization's performance and success in relation to its goals and objectives. These indicators are essential for decision-making and tracking progress.

Some common examples of KPIs include:

  • Conversion rate
  • Cost per acquisition
  • Customer response time
  • Return on investment (ROI)
  • Cart abandonment rate

KPIs should be specific, measurable, achievable, relevant, and timely (SMART) to be effective. Additionally, they should be regularly monitored to identify areas for improvement and take corrective actions.

In conclusion, KPIs are critical for evaluating an organization's performance and ensuring its long-term success. By selecting and measuring the right indicators, companies can identify opportunities for improvement and make informed decisions to achieve their goals.
